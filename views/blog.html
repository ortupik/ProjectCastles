Creating Real time application preview

October 18, 2016 by Chris Kipruto,CS DeKut

Though we emulate most of our systems from such applications,our interests rests solely on the fact that for us to even remotely be able to build the systems we aspire to create,we must be able to build such basic mock applications(such as this messaging app) that will act as a benchmark for our future real time applications.We are learners in this field, and a great a part of what we do is on a first time attempt or encounter.We are continuously learning a lot from this. We chose a messaging system as a prototype because it does hold the same basic principles that are in line with our venture of a robust,secure and reliable data transmission that should have(if implemented well) the propensity of a zero tolerance to failure or a barely, minimalistic failure rate , external factors non-inclusive. And to the basic fact that building one is essentially a means to learn new real time software technologies and it is fun and exciting.

Once we are through we would have a better estimation or view, of the possibility to make out the realization of our upcoming projects, and the necessary knowledge and working experience to undertake or advance in similar endevours.
